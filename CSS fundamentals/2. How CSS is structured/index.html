What is cortisol?

Cortisol is a hormone that governs your hunger cravings, digestion, blood pressure, sleep/wake patterns, physical activity, and capacity to cope with stress. It belongs to the glucocorticoid family – a fancy name for substances that can raise your glucose. This is cortisol’s main job: to increase your glucose and store the excess in the liver, through a process called glycogen storage. Glucose gives you energy. If your cells don’t get enough, you wilt.

As the most potent of glucocorticoids, cortisol keeps us alive via three key properties:

  • raises blood sugar
  • increases blood pressure
  • modulates inflammation
